# Motive — Product Vision

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Draft

**Project Name:** Motive

**Tagline:** *Know what matters next.*

---

# 1. Introduction

## Overview

Motive is an AI-powered execution companion designed to help people achieve meaningful goals by intelligently planning, prioritizing and adapting to the changing realities of daily life.

Unlike traditional productivity applications that simply store tasks or reminders, Motive continuously analyzes goals, commitments, schedules and new information to recommend the best next action.

Motive behaves like a trusted Chief of Staff that understands not only **what** needs to be done, but also **why**, **when**, and **what impact** each decision will have.

---

# 2. Problem Statement

Modern productivity tools suffer from several fundamental problems.

They primarily act as passive storage systems.

Users are responsible for:

* deciding priorities
* planning schedules
* reorganizing work
* resolving conflicts
* remembering deadlines

Existing tools answer questions like:

* What tasks do I have?
* What meetings are today?
* What is due tomorrow?

They rarely answer:

* What should I actually work on next?
* Which decision creates the biggest impact?
* What changed today that affects my plans?
* What is putting my goals at risk?

As a result, users spend considerable mental effort managing their productivity system instead of making meaningful progress.

---

# 3. Vision

Motive aims to become an intelligent execution layer above a user's digital life.

Rather than replacing existing tools such as Google Calendar or Gmail, Motive connects them, understands them, and continuously transforms information into actionable guidance.

The product should reduce decision fatigue by helping users focus on the highest impact work at any moment.

---

# 4. Mission

Help people spend less time planning and more time executing.

Every recommendation should increase confidence that the user is working on the right thing at the right time.

---

# 5. Core Philosophy

Motive is built upon five principles.

## 5.1 Goals define direction

Goals represent long-term desired outcomes.

Examples:

* Get France Visa
* Prepare for Promotion
* Crack Interview
* Buy a House

Goals define purpose.

Goals do not own time.

---

## 5.2 Commitments consume time

Anything occupying time is a Commitment.

Examples:

* Interview
* Team Meeting
* Passport Scan
* Workout
* Focus Block
* Doctor Appointment

Commitments may support Goals.

Commitments may also exist independently.

---

## 5.3 Calendar is the source of truth

Google Calendar owns scheduling.

Motive owns decision making.

The application should never attempt to replace Google Calendar.

Instead it should continuously enhance it.

---

## 5.4 AI must explain itself

Every recommendation must answer:

* Why now?
* Why this?
* What happens if I ignore it?
* What benefit will I gain?

Explainability is a first-class feature.

---

## 5.5 Simplicity over complexity

Users should never feel overwhelmed.

The interface should always prioritize clarity over feature density.

---

# 8. Success Criteria

Users should be able to answer the following within five seconds of opening the application:

1. What changed?
2. What should I do now?
3. Why is this important?
4. Am I on track?
5. What is my biggest risk?

If these questions are answered, the dashboard has succeeded.

# 9. Core Concepts

## Goal

Represents a desired outcome.

Goals contain:

* progress
* execution score
* risk
* success metrics

Goals are never directly scheduled.

---

## Commitment

Represents anything consuming time.

Commitments include:

* Events
* Tasks
* Focus Blocks
* Meetings
* Appointments

Commitments may optionally relate to one or more Goals.

---

## Relationship

Relationships connect Goals and Commitments.

Relationships may be:

* user-defined
* AI-discovered
* imported

Relationships should include confidence levels.

---

## Recommendation

Recommendations represent the AI's current understanding of the highest-value action.

Every recommendation contains:

* reason
* impact
* estimated duration
* confidence
* priority

---

## Focus Block

A Focus Block represents reserved execution time.

Rather than permanently assigning one task, the AI may dynamically choose the most appropriate Commitment for that block.

This enables adaptive scheduling while minimizing calendar disruption.

# 11. Explicitly Out of Scope

The MVP intentionally excludes:

* Voice Assistant
* Team Collaboration
* Mobile Applications
* Gamification
* AI Memory
* Habit Tracking
* Slack Integration
* Jira Integration
* GitHub Integration
* What-If Simulator

These remain future enhancements.

---

# 12. User Experience

Opening Motive should feel like sitting down with a highly organized executive assistant.

The application should immediately surface:

* important changes
* scheduling conflicts
* recommended actions
* risks
* progress

The user should leave the dashboard knowing exactly what deserves attention.

---

# 13. Long-Term Vision

Motive should evolve into an intelligent operating layer that understands a user's goals, commitments and digital environment.

Future integrations may include:

* Google Drive
* Google Docs
* Slack
* GitHub
* Jira
* Apple Calendar
* Microsoft Outlook
* Fitness Devices
* Email Providers

The core philosophy should remain unchanged:

> Help users consistently make the best next decision with minimal cognitive effort.

---

# 14. Guiding Principle

Every feature added to Motive should answer one question:

> **Does this help the user understand what matters next?**

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.
